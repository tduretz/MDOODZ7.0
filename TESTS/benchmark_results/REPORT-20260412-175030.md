## Key Findings

1. **Optimal thread count is 6–8** for production runs on this 16-vCPU machine. Beyond 8 threads, performance degrades due to serial thermal solver and OpenMP overhead.

2. **Thermal solver is the primary bottleneck** at medium-to-high resolution, consuming 6.3–6.8s per step at 1000×800 regardless of thread count. This is the CHOLMOD direct solver and is inherently serial. Parallelising or replacing this solver would yield the largest performance gain.

3. **Rheology and advection scale well** with threads (near-linear speedup up to 4–6 threads), confirming they are properly OpenMP-parallelised.

4. **Assembly does not scale** — it remains near-constant or increases slightly with threads, suggesting contention or serial sections in the stiffness matrix assembly.

5. **Memory is predictable** at ~15.5 MB per 1000 grid cells, independent of thread count. A 2000×1600 run would require ~50 GB RAM.

6. **Grid scaling is linear** ($O(n)$) — doubling resolution in each dimension (4× cells) costs ~4× wall time, as expected for an explicit marker-in-cell scheme.

## Recommendations

- **For this machine**: Use `--threads 6` or `--threads 8` for best throughput.
- **For larger grids**: Prioritise thermal solver optimisation (iterative solver, multigrid preconditioner, or parallel direct solver like PARDISO).
- **For benchmarking**: 10 steps is sufficient — per-step cost is stable and representative.
- **For cloud cost optimisation**: c5ad.4xlarge (16 vCPU) is oversized given 6–8 thread saturation. A c5ad.2xlarge (8 vCPU) would be more cost-effective, or use a memory-optimised instance (r-series) for highres runs that need 12+ GB.
